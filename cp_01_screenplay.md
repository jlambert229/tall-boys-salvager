# Salvager

## Chapter 1 (screenplay draft)

**Based on:** Introduction to the Mech Warrior Who Never Was

**Voiceover convention:** `BAM (V.O.)` is **internal monologue** (Bam's thoughts). Other characters do not hear it. Spoken lines use `BAM` without `(V.O.)`.

---

FADE IN:

EXT. DEAD-END ALLEY - YORP - TWILIGHT

Rocket-scarred towers lean over a canyon of rubble. Murals fight for wall space with neon bubble letters nobody can decode.

A MECHANIZED SALVAGE RIG sits patched and patient. Beside it: SKETCH, a construction-class biped, seven meters of grease-streaked metal. Camera eyes catch the last sword-stroke of sun from below the horizon.

For a beat Sketch seems wreathed in flame. He is not on fire. He is very still.

SKETCH  
It's getting dark.

BAM (V.O.)  
If ten tons of gears and kilometers of fiber optic could keep a secret, maybe this is what he would look like imagining the end. That is no way to think about your only friend.

Bameron Kold, nineteen, BAM, wipes a smear off a hydraulic line.

BAM (V.O.)  
Salvage tools. Salvage nerves.

BAM (V.O.)  
The sun should not bounce off his eyes like that. Not from this angle. Machines calculate. They do not think. They do not feel. They are not friends. That is what I tell myself.

Sketch looms. Bam is dwarfed even beside his own rig.

BAM (V.O.)  
My rig does not have a name. The robot does. Sketch. Me and Sketch, best friends for life. He lifts. He digs me out when structures fold. Sometimes he breaks things. I pick where we go. The trick is to haul enough metal to matter without getting killed-to-death by soldiers, gang bosses, or gravity.

Bam squints at the skyline: broken teeth on the horizon.

BAM (V.O.)  
High-rises lean like they want to whisper. Mostly they are sad about holes and tags. Art is art. Some of my friends still need their spray cans thrown at the sun.

SKETCH  
Darkness. It is descending, as I have quite clearly mentioned.

BAM  
That happens about the same time each day.

He looks toward the broken horizon.

BAM  
Some people blame it on the setting sun.

Sketch holds. Attuned.

BAM (V.O.)  
Not every bot bothers with small talk.

BAM  
I'll protect you, dude.

SKETCH  
I am not a dude. I am made of metal and need zero protection from a small, squishy biological like you.

BAM  
Whatever, Metal Dude.

SKETCH  
Please do not change my name. Sketch is a fine identifier, though the reason you applied it to me remains beyond logic.

BAM  
Maybe you have two names.

SKETCH  
Of course that would make sense.

BAM (V.O.)  
Dry as one of my more sarcastic uncles.

BAM (V.O.)  
Robots: blessing or pain in the ass. Right now, bulk reads as safe. I have a lot on my mind.

Sketch's plating carries BLACK SLIME. Threads of RED in it.

BAM (V.O.)  
Greased up. Blood in the mix. Neither of us knew where it was from. Or who.

BAM (V.O.)  
I did not dig for a face I would recognize. A soldier would be worse, for other reasons. Collapses do not leave bodies to bury. One day you are here. The next you are concrete. No goodbyes. No shoe-size fights. Everything here is damaged, including my people.

Bam checks the rig: seals, hydraulics.

BAM (V.O.)  
Anything that screams tomorrow.

BAM (V.O.)  
I wish for a car wash. Nobody knows what a car was. Sometimes you find a bay with a hose that still spits. "Car Wash." "Wash-o-World." Almost never works. Uncle Iton knows places with power, pressure, even air conditioning. Elders brag about how hard they had it, then brag about miracles. Happyville.

CUT TO:

INT. COMMUNITY FIRE RING - YORP - LAST NIGHT

Faces in firelight. UNCLE ITON holds court. Kids on laps. Adults passing something that is not quite hope.

ITON  
Vaux VI struggled for the first hundred years of colonization, a key period for each new world.

He coughs for effect.

ITON  
After that, it was, as the ancients sometimes said, on like Donkey Kong.

Murmurs. Someone mouths "donkey." Someone mouths "kong."

ITON  
War broke out, and every soul on the atmospherically friendly space rock cleared their calendars for two hundred years. The yellow sun rose and fell without passing judgment. To some, a sad deity watching her favorite child march toward needless self-destruction.

He slides a look toward Bam.

ITON  
To others, every day was a laugh riot full of friends, shenanigans, and bold adventures, because that was the only sane way to survive with any sort of quality of life. Reminds me of someone we all know. Rhymes with slam.

Chuckles. Knowing glances at Bam.

BAM (V.O.)  
They call me Bameron Kold. Bam. Reckless. Hardworking. Ridiculously good-looking. Funny. Accessible. Yorp is one hop from damnation. Nineteen years old, nineteen years in contested zones. I am not scared to fight. Lately I hear a higher calling anyway.

ITON  
The rigs did not make themselves. There was a natural progression. Problems got solved. When the sun goes down, we live with the rewards of our ingenuity.

BAM (V.O.)  
Humans flinch when rounds punch through their faces. Two centuries of war. Better attack. Better defense. Lately that means C-FEF. Mech rigs. Air support. Same species, new fleet. War makers. Dream takers.

ITON  
Capian First Expeditionary Fleet. Does anyone know what that means?

Silence. He does not fill it.

BAM (V.O.)  
Other nights it is UPV or ROV6. Acronyms without owners. Iton thinks V is for Vaux. He stops when the trail goes cold. Because he didn't know. My exhausted mind keeps hitting that revelation. Contrary to what he wanted everyone to believe, he didn't know shit about sunshine, as Grams liked to snicker.

ITON  
Infantry was needed to take and hold territory. That was the only way to claim true victory. Good habit: take what is yours. Deny those who would deny you.

Bam's attention drifts. He doodles on a last good pad.

ITON  
Exoskeletons that let each soldier carry more guns, bullets, and contraband pop-tarts had to be armored. Weapons upgraded. Fighting intensified.

The KIDS laugh at "pop-tarts." Bam draws a robot dinosaur eating pastries. TOMMY and SAMANTHA smother giggles. Nobody interrupts Iton.

ITON  
That led to modern-day battle knights stomping across the ruined landscape in mechanized combat rigs five meters high.

BACK TO:

EXT. DEAD-END ALLEY - YORP - TWILIGHT

Bam measures the world with his heel, toe, heel.

BAM (V.O.)  
Meters to feet calms me. Scavengers see bad things. I am supposed to say salvager. Legal word. Looting gets you shot. C-FEF, ROV6, UPV, same rule: do not strip their dead. I get it. If Iton went down and I saw a stranger in his boots without a word, I would lose it.

He compares his foot to Sketch. To the rig.

BAM  
If I am making up the rules, then I am making up the rules.

He scrubs grease off Sketch.

BAM (V.O.)  
I try not to think about what greased us in the last collapse. Rats. Shadows. Anything but a name.

BAM (V.O.)  
C-FEF calls their rigs MCRs. I eavesdropped once from rubble they did not search. Mine is an MSR. Mechanize salvaging rig. Not cool. Maybe Iton can brand it.

SKETCH  
What in the name of all that is rational are you talking about? Rules? You are making up rules now?

BAM  
Meters versus feet.

SKETCH  
What?

BAM  
I think it would be way better to measure things like this.

He walks heel to toe, hands framing the distance.

BAM  
Uncle Iton, and about everyone else, use some made-up system from the star travelers who brought us here. My feet are more reliable.

Sketch half-hides in the crooked bones of a building frame. Tinny voice from shadow.

SKETCH  
If you say so. I understand how biologicals need to feel there is such a thing as destiny, and that they have some meager influence on its course.

BAM  
Now you sound like Uncle Iton.

SKETCH  
Is that a compliment?

Bam answers with a noncommittal grunt. Sketch does not push.

BAM (V.O.)  
Seven meters. Twenty-three of my feet. Seven feet taller than my rig, math willing. Five meters of war machine versus seven of construction steel can mean everything or nothing, depending on the job. Small can dodge what big cannot.

CRACK-CRACK-CRACK. TRACER ROUNDS whip over a gutted TRAIN STATION roof. Bam freezes. Sketch: silent protocol.

BAM (V.O.)  
Nothing follows. No treads. No MCR footfalls. Random Tuesday in Yorp. Someone fighting somewhere else. Those were misses. My sector goes quiet wrong.

Bam gives up on the last of the gunk. Packs tools.

BAM  
Let's get moving before shots are fired at us.

Sketch steps into the mouth of the alley. Scans. Lingers toward the tracer origin. Ten seconds. Turns back.

SKETCH  
We should have nearly three hours before this sector heats up, if the pattern holds. All is well, Bam.

BAM  
Our goal is to find one new thing tonight. Do you think we can manage that?

SKETCH  
You are making the rules. You tell me.

Bam climbs the rig. Straps in. Opaque canopy CLANGS shut. Monitors flutter on. A fan pushes cool air against his face.

BAM (V.O.)  
Maybe it wasn't air conditioning, but it didn't suck.

BAM  
Tonight is going to be the best yet. Trust me.

He throttles. The rig rises. Sketch falls in.

FADE OUT.

END OF CHAPTER 1 (SCREENPLAY DRAFT)
